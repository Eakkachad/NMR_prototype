# Writing Standards — Proposal & Deliverables

## General Principles

1. **Every sentence must earn its space.** The proposal is 2 pages max.
2. **Lead with impact, follow with method.** Judges are not all technical experts.
3. **Concrete over vague.** Numbers > adjectives. "Reduces time by 95%" > "Significantly faster."
4. **Medical personnel perspective.** Always ask: "Would a doctor understand this?"

## Tone
- Professional but accessible
- Confident but honest (especially about novel/unproven techniques)
- Solution-oriented

## Structure Rules
- Use short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Use bullet points for lists
- Use tables for comparisons
- Bold key terms on first use
- No orphan headings (always have content after a heading)

## Technical Language Guidelines

### DO:
- "Our pipeline automatically classifies compounds from NMR signals"
- "We propose a novel application of Neural ODEs for spectral alignment"
- "The baseline model achieves >85% classification accuracy"
- "Reduces interpretation time from 4 hours to under 5 minutes"

### DON'T:
- ~~"We leverage a cutting-edge AI paradigm"~~ (vague buzzwords)
- ~~"Our Neural ODE has been proven effective for NMR"~~ (overclaiming)
- ~~"The system performs well"~~ (no metrics)
- ~~"This is the first-ever solution"~~ (unverifiable)

## Citation Style
- Use author-year in text: (Zhou et al., 2025)
- Full reference not needed in 2-page proposal (space constraint)
- Only cite papers that directly support a claim

## Success Metrics Guidance

Good metrics:
- "Classify 3 compound types with >85% F1 score"
- "Reduce NMR interpretation from 4 hours to <5 minutes per sample"
- "Enable non-specialist operators to perform metabolomics screening"
- "Process 100 samples per hour (vs. 1 sample per 4 hours manually)"

Bad metrics:
- ~~"Improve accuracy"~~ (by how much?)
- ~~"Make it easier"~~ (how is ease measured?)
- ~~"Help medical personnel"~~ (what specific help?)

## Workflow Diagram Standards
- Use clear boxes and arrows
- Color-code by stage (input → preprocessing → ML → output)
- Label every arrow
- Include both Core and Advanced paths
- Must be readable at the printed page size

## Thai vs. English
- Primary working language: English (for AI agent efficiency)
- Submission language: Match competition requirements (may need Thai)
- If Thai required: translate only the final version, not working drafts
